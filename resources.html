resources

things i've actually used to get into quant. no filler. if it's on here it either shaped how i think or i actively reference it. i get asked about this enough that it made sense to write it down.

interview prep 1 book
  • A Practical Guide to Quantitative Finance Interviews Xinfeng Zhou
    interview the green book. covers probability, brain teasers, calculus, linear algebra, options pricing, basically the full sweep of what quant interviews actually test. start here before anything else if you have an interview coming up. work through every problem, don't just read. ↗
options & volatility 4 books
  • Trading Volatility Colin Bennett
    vol trading free PDF on his site. the most complete treatment of vol trading i've found: skew, term structure, dispersion, variance swaps. dense but worth it. i keep coming back to it. if you're going anywhere near a vol desk this is required reading. ↗
  • Option Market Making Allen Jan Baird
    market making older book but one of the few that actually explains how market makers think about inventory, risk, and edge. less about formulas and more about the mental model. good complement to natenberg. ↗
  • Option Volatility & Pricing Sheldon Natenberg
    options the standard. if you're going into options or vol this is the bible. natenberg builds intuition before throwing math at you, which is the right order. read it cover to cover once, then use it as a reference. ↗
  • Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives John C. Hull
    derivatives hull's textbook. used in basically every derivatives course. not something you read cover to cover. it's a reference. strong on the mechanics of futures, swaps, and options pricing. useful to have around. ↗
mathematics 5 books
  • Stochastic Calculus for Finance I Steven E. Shreve
    stochastic calc the standard intro to stochastic calculus for quant finance. covers discrete-time models, brownian motion, and the foundations you need for options pricing theory. volume I is the cleaner starting point. get comfortable here before jumping to volume II. ↗
  • Understanding Analysis Stephen Abbott
    real analysis the best real analysis intro i've found. cleaner and more readable than rudin. abbott actually explains the motivation behind every definition instead of just stating it. if your analysis foundations are shaky, start here. ↗
  • Linear Algebra Done Right Sheldon Axler
    linear algebra axler deliberately avoids determinants until the end and builds everything from first principles. builds real intuition for what linear maps actually are. better for theory than strang. if you want to actually understand what's happening underneath PCA, covariance matrices, etc., this is the book. ↗
  • Bayesian Statistics the Fun Way Will Kurt
    statistics not rigorous but that's the point. good for building bayesian intuition if you've been thinking frequentist your whole life. read it fast, then move to something heavier if you need to go deeper. the mental model shift is worth the afternoon it takes. ↗
  • Fifty Challenging Problems in Probability Frederick Mosteller
    probability classic collection. short problems with elegant solutions, the kind you see in quant interviews. good for building the probabilistic intuition that textbooks don't actually give you. work through them without looking at the answers first. ↗
general 1 book
  • One Up on Wall Street Peter Lynch
    investing not quant at all, but lynch's framework for how to think about companies and markets is worth understanding even if you're building systematic strategies. it's a good reminder that markets are made of real businesses, not just data streams. easy read. ↗
practice sites 5 sites
  • quantguide.io
    interview prep the best structured resource for quant interview practice. covers probability, statistics, options, and mental math with difficulty ratings. i used this heavily before interviews. do it consistently, not in one panic session. ↗
  • anazac.io
    market making focused on market making and trading puzzles. good for understanding edge, pricing under uncertainty, and the adversarial dynamics of quote-driven markets. less covered by other resources so worth checking out. ↗
  • tradermath.org
    mental math pure mental math speed practice. underrated. fast mental arithmetic matters more in interviews than people expect. do timed sessions. it's repetitive but that's the point. ↗
  • gurmeet.net/puzzles
    puzzles collection of classic math and logic puzzles. great for building the combinatorial and lateral thinking that shows up in brain teaser rounds. some of these are legitimately hard. don't look at the answers too fast. ↗
  • puzzledquant.com
    puzzles quant-specific puzzles with a focus on probability and statistics. good complement to gurmeet. the problems here are more finance-adjacent. useful for filling in gaps after quantguide. ↗
