about me

I’m a researcher and student studying Applied Mathematics & Finance at De Anza College. I like working on ideas that bridge markets, data, and computation, then turning them into frameworks and simulations people can actually use. My work spans quantitative finance, private equity, and economic research, and I draw on both theory and hands-on programming to bring it to life.

I’ve built everything from reinforcement learning execution algorithms to full equity research reports and financial models, always aiming for rigor, scalability, and clarity. I enjoy connecting mathematics and market intuition to solve tough problems in practical, measurable ways.

Most recently, I was at a Stealth Hedge Fund in NYC working on mortgage-backed securities (MBS), where I focused on option-adjusted spread modeling, convexity risk, and execution research in fixed income markets as a quantitative researcher. Whatever the project, I aim to design systems that are not only intellectually sound, but also actionable, resilient, and ready for real-world application.

background

I grew up in San Francisco and Seattle, where I developed a fascination for mathematics and systems. My early projects ranged from working on solar flare projects in astrophysics to reselling sneakers and building a business in high school. Over time, I gravitated toward quantitative research & financial modeling, which led me to internships, co-founding research groups, and bridging theory with practical investments.

mission & values

I believe in curiosity, rigor, and connecting across domains. My work is powered by asking why repeatedly, building from first principles, and staying humble in the face of uncertainty. I also value transparency, mentorship, and continuous learning.
