About

I am a student studying Applied Mathematics & Finance at De Anza College, focused on quantitative finance and market-driven research. I work at the intersection of mathematical theory, data, and systems, translating abstract ideas (stochastic models, optimization problems, probabilistic assumptions, etc.) into tools that hold up under real market constraints.

My interests center on derivatives, fixed income, prediction markets, and market microstructure, where small modeling assumptions can materially alter outcomes. I am particularly drawn to market making and execution problems, including liquidity provision, order-flow dynamics, and pricing under uncertainty. I care deeply about rigor, interpretability, and building systems that scale beyond toy examples while remaining execution-aware.

I am currently part of the Life Sciences Investment Banking team at KCC Capital Partners, working on pharma and biotech M&A advisory with an emphasis on valuation, diligence, and strategic positioning. My responsibilities include building DCF and comparable analyses, evaluating clinical-stage and commercial drug assets, and integrating regulatory, pipeline, and scientific risk into transaction frameworks. I support sell-side and strategic processes by translating complex market and clinical dynamics into financial narratives used in live deal execution.

Previously, I worked at a hedge fund in New York conducting structured credit research across CMOs and mortgage derivatives, with a focus on cash-flow waterfall modeling, option-adjusted spread analytics, and convexity-driven risk decomposition. My work emphasized rate–prepayment optionality, tranche-level relative value, and execution-aware modeling within fixed-income portfolios.

Across roles and independent research, my goal is to design quantitative models and market systems that bridge theory and PnL, whether in derivatives pricing, market making, or prediction markets—delivering research and tools that are robust, scalable, and built for real-world deployment.

Background

I grew up between Seattle and San Francisco, where I developed early interests in both business and physics, drawn to how complex systems behave and how incentives shape outcomes. After moving to the Bay Area, mathematics emerged as the unifying language that connected these interests, giving me a more precise way to reason about uncertainty, structure, and scale. That progression led me from astrophysics research on solar flares, to running a small resale business, and ultimately to quantitative finance and financial modeling. Each step reinforced the same idea: well-designed systems matter, and small details compound over time.

Mission and values

I value intellectual curiosity, careful reasoning, and clarity under uncertainty. I try to ask why as often as possible, build from first principles, and pressure-test assumptions rather than narratives. I care about designing models and systems that are interpretable, extensible, and useful to others—not just technically correct. This means writing clearly, exposing assumptions, and building work that can be understood, critiqued, and improved upon over time.
