bay area + seattle

📍 split between two places

i'm based in cupertino, which puts me squarely in the heart of the bay. most of my work happens here or up in SF — i'm in the city often enough that it functions as a second home even if it isn't technically where i sleep. the bay has a particular energy that's hard to describe to people who haven't lived in it: ambition is the ambient baseline, not the exception. you absorb that without trying.

SF specifically is one of the strangest and most interesting cities i know. dense enough to feel inexhaustible, small enough to feel walkable, with a fog that rolls in every evening like it's on a timer. it has more contradictions per square mile than anywhere else — wealth against poverty, technology optimism against civic dysfunction. the narrative that it's "over" is mostly written by people who don't actually spend time there.

seattle is home in the original sense — where i grew up, where my family is, where i go during breaks. it's a completely different pace. greener, quieter, wetter. the mountains are right there. it makes the bay feel loud in comparison, which is sometimes exactly what you need to remember.

i think being between two places keeps you from becoming too anchored to either one's particular way of seeing things, which is useful.
